# Debt Management

## Understanding Debt

Debt is money you owe to creditors. Understanding your debt is the first step to managing it effectively.

### Types of Debt

**Good Debt** (typically lower interest rates):
- Mortgages (home loans)
- Student loans
- Business loans

**Bad Debt** (typically higher interest rates):
- Credit card debt
- Personal loans
- Payday loans

## Creating a Debt Repayment Plan

### Step 1: List All Debts

Write down:
- Creditor name
- Total amount owed
- Interest rate
- Monthly payment
- Minimum payment

### Step 2: Choose a Strategy

**Debt Avalanche Method**
- Pay minimums on all debts
- Put extra money toward highest interest rate debt
- Most mathematically efficient
- Saves the most money on interest

**Debt Snowball Method**
- Pay minimums on all debts
- Put extra money toward smallest debt
- More motivating emotionally
- Provides quick wins

### Step 3: Negotiate with Creditors

- Ask about lower interest rates
- Request payment plan modifications
- Consolidate debts if it lowers total interest
- Avoid settling for less unless as last resort

## Staying Debt-Free

### Prevention Strategies

1. **Use cash for discretionary spending**: Physical cash makes you more aware of spending
2. **Avoid impulse purchases**: Wait 24 hours before buying non-essentials
3. **Pay credit cards in full**: Avoid interest charges
4. **Don't borrow for depreciating assets**: Cars, clothes, gadgets lose value quickly
5. **Build an emergency fund**: Prevent taking on debt for emergencies

### Building Good Credit

- Pay bills on time
- Keep credit card balances low
- Don't close old credit accounts
- Limit new credit applications
- Regularly check your credit report
