# Budgeting Basics

## Creating a Budget

A budget is a plan for your money. It helps you understand where your money goes and ensures you have enough for your needs.

### Steps to Create a Budget

1. **Track your income**: Add up all money you receive each month
2. **List your expenses**: Write down everything you spend money on
3. **Categorize expenses**: Group expenses into categories like housing, food, transportation
4. **Set spending limits**: Decide how much to spend in each category
5. **Monitor and adjust**: Review your budget monthly and make changes as needed

### The 50/30/20 Rule

A simple budgeting approach:
- **50%** for needs (housing, food, utilities, transportation)
- **30%** for wants (entertainment, dining out, hobbies)
- **20%** for savings and debt repayment

## Emergency Fund

An emergency fund is money set aside for unexpected expenses. Aim to save 3-6 months of living expenses.

### Why You Need It

- Car repairs
- Medical expenses
- Job loss
- Home repairs
- Unexpected bills

Start small if you can't save much. Even €500 can help with minor emergencies.

## Expense Tracking

### Tools for Tracking

- Spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets)
- Mobile apps (Mint, YNAB, Expense Manager)
- Bank apps built-in tracking
- Pen and paper

### How Often to Track

- Daily: Review transactions made that day
- Weekly: Categorize and summarize weekly spending
- Monthly: Review against budget and adjust as needed

Consistent tracking helps you understand spending patterns and identify areas to cut back.
